Picture Fixer

Picture Fixer crops and adjusts a single photo and saves it as a 1800×1013 pixel JPEG, ready to upload to WordPress.

1. Upload your image

Click the upload area, drag a photo file onto it, or paste with ⌘V (Mac) / Ctrl+V (Windows).

Accepted formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, TIFF, BMP, HEIC.

2. Crop

  • Drag the crop box to frame the part of the image you want to keep.
  • Drag from the edges or corners to reposition — the 16:9 ratio stays locked.
  • Use the − and + buttons at the top right of the crop area to zoom out and in.

3. Brightness, contrast and sharpness

  • Click + Enhance to increase brightness and contrast, or − Reduce to decrease. Each click adjusts by 10%. The badge in the middle shows the current level.
  • Click Sharpen to add crispness to the image. The button turns blue when active. Click again to turn it off.

4. Caption and download

  • Type a caption in the text field. The original filename of the uploaded photo is shown above the field in small grey text — use it as a reference if the filename contains the subject’s name.
  • The output filename is generated automatically from your caption and today’s date, shown in the preview next to the button.
  • Click Download fixed image.
Portrait Fixer

Portrait Fixer combines 2–6 portrait photos side by side into a single 1800×1013 pixel JPEG, with a white dividing line between each photo.

1. Switch to Portrait Fixer and upload your photos

Click Portrait Fixer in the top navigation bar. Then click the upload area or drag 2–6 photos onto it. You can upload them all at once or one at a time. Once the first photo loads, an Add another portrait area appears at the bottom of the page.

2. Crop, adjust and name each portrait

Each photo gets its own panel. In each panel:

  • Drag and zoom the crop box the same way as in Picture Fixer.
  • + Enhance, − Reduce and Sharpen work the same as in Picture Fixer.
  • Type the person’s name in the Person’s name field. The original filename is shown above as a reference.
The crop frame shape adjusts automatically if you add or remove a portrait, because the width of each slot changes with the number of photos. Check your crops again after adding or removing anyone.

3. Reorder the portraits

Grab the ⋮⋮ handle at the top left of any panel and drag it up or down to change the order. The composite preview at the bottom of the page updates automatically.

4. Download

  • The composite preview shows how the final image will look.
  • Every Person’s name field must be filled before the download button becomes active.
  • Click Download composite. The filename is made from all the names in order, separated by underscores, plus today’s date.
